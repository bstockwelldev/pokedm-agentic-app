# Battle Rules
status: Mandatory

## Turn Structure
Battles proceed in turns. Each turn, the active trainer selects an action: move, switch, item, or flee.
Speed determines move order unless a priority modifier applies.
Higher speed goes first. Ties are resolved randomly.

## Damage Calculation
Damage = ((2 × Level / 5 + 2) × Power × Atk / Def) / 50 + 2
Apply type effectiveness multiplier (0.5×, 1×, 2×) after base damage.
Apply STAB (Same Type Attack Bonus) of 1.5× if the move type matches the user's type.
Apply random variance: 85–100% rolled each hit.

## Type Effectiveness
Super effective (2×): doubles damage.
Not very effective (0.5×): halves damage.
Immune (0×): move has no effect.
Multiple type interactions multiply (e.g., 2× × 2× = 4× for dual-type targets).

## Status Conditions
Burn: halves physical Attack; deals 1/16 max HP at end of each turn.
Paralysis: reduces Speed by 50%; 25% chance of full paralysis (skips turn).
Poison: deals 1/8 max HP at end of each turn.
Badly Poisoned: deals 1/16 HP turn 1, increasing by 1/16 each subsequent turn.
Sleep: cannot act for 1–3 turns. Wakes naturally or via items.
Freeze: cannot act. 20% chance to thaw each turn. Always thaws when hit by fire moves.
Confusion: 33% chance to hit self for 40 base power typeless damage.

## Fainting
A Pokémon faints when HP reaches 0. It cannot battle until healed.
Custom Pokémon and canon Pokémon always faint — they never die.
A trainer loses the battle when all their Pokémon have fainted.

## Switching
A trainer may switch Pokémon instead of using a move on their turn.
On a switch-in, entry hazards (Stealth Rock, Spikes, etc.) activate.
Emergency switches (after fainting) do not cost a turn.

## Items in Battle
Each item use consumes the trainer's turn.
Poké Balls may only be used in wild encounters, not trainer battles.
Max one held item per Pokémon.
